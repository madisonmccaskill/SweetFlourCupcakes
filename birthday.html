Classic Birthday Cupcakes

Light, fluffy, and topped with a swirl of colorful buttercream, these cupcakes are the perfect treat for a birthday celebration. Whether you are baking for kids, teens, or adults, they are guaranteed to please the crowd!

Prep Time: 15 min
Bake Time: 20 min
Total Time: 35 min
Yields: 12 cupcakes

For the Cupcakes

  • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 ¼ teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • ¼ cup sour cream
  • Optional: ¼ cup rainbow sprinkles

For the Buttercream Frosting

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream or milk
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Food coloring (optional)
  • Sprinkles for topping (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F. Line a 12-cup muffin tin with cupcake liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the butter and sugar with an electric mixer on medium-high until light and fluffy (about 2–3 minutes).
  4. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in the vanilla extract.
  5. Mix in the sour cream. Then alternate adding the dry ingredients and milk, beginning and ending with the dry mix. Mix until just combined.
  6. Gently fold in rainbow sprinkles if making funfetti-style cupcakes.
  7. Divide the batter evenly among the liners (about ¾ full). Bake for 18–20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Let cupcakes cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely before frosting.

Make the Frosting

  1. Beat butter on medium-high speed until creamy (about 3 minutes).
  2. Gradually add powdered sugar (1 cup at a time), mixing on low until combined.
  3. Add vanilla, salt, and cream 1 tablespoon at a time, beating until fluffy.
  4. Optional: divide into bowls and tint with food coloring for a rainbow swirl.
  5. Pipe or spread onto cooled cupcakes and top with sprinkles.

Helpful Tips!

  • For bakery-style swirls, use a large piping tip.
  • Make sure your butter is fully softened for smooth batter and frosting.
  • Do not overmix the batter — mix just until combined to keep the cupcakes fluffy.
  • Let cupcakes cool completely before frosting to prevent melting.
  • Store frosted cupcakes in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days.
  • Bring refrigerated cupcakes to room temperature before serving for best flavor and texture.
